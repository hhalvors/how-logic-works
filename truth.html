How Logic Works: Exercises

Hans Halvorson

Truth

Exercise 5.1 Write the truth table for P ∧ ¬P. How do you interpret the significance of the result?

Exercise 5.2 Compute the truth-table for ¬P ↔︎ ¬Q. How do you interpret the significance of the result?

Exercise 5.3 Write truth-tables for ¬P ∨ Q and P → Q, and say whether either one implies the other.

Exercise 5.4 Show that P cannot be derived from P ↔︎ Q.

Exercise 5.5 Explain why P ∧ ¬P ⊢ Q is truth-preserving.

Exercise 5.6 Show that if  ⊢ ϕ ↔︎ ψ then ϕ ⊣  ⊢ ψ.

Exercise 5.7 Provide counterexamples to the following invalid argument forms.

  1. P → Q, Q ⊢ P

  2. P → R ⊢ (P∨Q) → R

  3. P → R ⊢ P → (Q∧R)

  4. P → (Q→R)⊢ (P→Q) → R
